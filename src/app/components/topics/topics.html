What are the main topics in the surveys?

After analyzing survey results, the Office of Research and Evaluation categorized each question into one of five general topics. For each topic a score is constructed by calculating the % of responses to topic-related questions that are the best possible choice. On this page you can choose a topic to compare schools and other groups of students for each of these topics.

School Climate refers to the attitudes, feelings, and interpersonal relationships associated with the school environment. Sub-topics include bullying, safety, attendance, discipline, and relationships.
Instruction refers to teaching and learning within the classroom and through assigned coursework. Sub-topics include teaching, student engagement, data usage, and district support.
Parent/Guardian-Community Ties refers to the connectedness of a school with its parents,guardians, and surrounding community. Sub-topics include outreach, communication quality, guardian involvement, and networks. These questions are asked of parent/guardianss, teachers, and principals.
School Leadership refers to decision making related to the school and the overall management of the building and its staff. Sub-topics include leadership decisions, feedback, and school focus priorities. These questions appear on the parent/guardian, teacher and principal surveys.
Professional Capacity refers to the cultivation and mastery of instructional practice within the school. Sub-topics include professional development, peer collaboration, and opportunities for innovation. These questions appear on the teacher and principal surveys only.

Select a respondent group and topic to view the average topic score by school on the map for {{$ctrl.CYTD}}. You can zoom or scroll the area of the map.

Select a respondent group and topic to view the average topic score by network. Bars display averages for {{$ctrl.CYTD}}. Circles display averages for {{$ctrl.PYTD}}. Each bar and circle represents a learning network. You can click on a network to see all schools in that network. For Student and Parent topics you may click on a school to show grade levels in that school.
