What are the main topics in the surveys?

Each question relates to one of five topics.

School Climate refers to the attitudes, feelings, and interpersonal relationships associated with the school environment. Subtopics include bullying, safety, attendance, discipline, and relationships. All respondents are asked questions about school climate.
Instruction refers to teaching and learning within the classroom and through assigned coursework. Subtopics include teaching, student engagement, data usage, and District support. All respondents are asked questions about school climate.
Parent/Guardian-Community Ties includes questions intended to capture the level of connection between a school, its parents/guardians, and its surrounding community. Subtopics include outreach, communication quality, guardian involvement, and networks. These questions appear on the parent/guardian, teacher, and principal surveys.
School Leadership refers to decision-making related to the school and the overall management of its building and staff. Subtopics include leadership decisions, feedback, and school focus priorities. These questions appear on the parent/guardian, teacher, and principal surveys.
Professional Capacity refers to the cultivation and mastery of instructional practice within the school. Subtopics include professional development, peer collaboration, and opportunities for innovation. These questions appear on the teacher and principal surveys only.

Click the “Topic Report” bar and select a school in the green bar above to see a radar chart and tables displaying survey feedback by topic and subtopic. These reports use topic and subtopic scores, which are numeric versions of survey responses. The higher the score, the more positive the response.

This report uses topic and subtopic average scores scaled from 0 to 10 (where 10 is the highest score).

Select a respondent group and topic to view the average topic score by network. Bars display averages for {{$ctrl.CYTD}}. Circles display averages for {{$ctrl.PYTD}}. Each bar and circle represents a learning network. You can click on a network to see all schools in that network. For student and parent topics, you can also click on a school to view the scores by grade level. You can switch your view to see scores on a 0-10 scale or “% most positive.” (“% most positive” is the percent of respondents that selected the best response.)
